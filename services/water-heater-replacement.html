When to Call for Water Heater Replacement

  • Your water heater is 10+ years old and showing signs of age
  • You notice rust-colored water or a metallic taste
  • Water isn't getting as hot as it used to
  • You see moisture or leaking around the base of the unit
  • You hear rumbling, banging, or other unusual noises
  • Your hot water runs out faster than before

Water Heater Replacement Questions

How long do water heaters typically last?

Traditional tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years with proper maintenance. If your water heater is approaching or past this age, replacement is usually more cost-effective than ongoing repairs.

How do I know what size water heater I need?

Water heater size depends on your household size and hot water usage. A family of 3-4 typically needs a 40-50 gallon tank. We'll assess your needs during our visit and recommend the right size for your home.

Are permits required for water heater replacement?

Yes, permits are required for water heater replacement in California. We handle all permit applications and ensure your installation meets local building codes and safety standards.

What warranty comes with a new water heater?

New water heaters come with manufacturer warranties ranging from 6-12 years depending on the model. We also guarantee our installation workmanship and will address any installation-related issues.

Do you remove and haul away the old water heater?

Yes, removal and proper disposal of your old water heater is included in our service. We'll take care of everything so you don't have to worry about it.
